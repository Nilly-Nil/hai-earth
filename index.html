About

What is h/ai?

h/ai is an independent Human–AI interaction research and transformation initiative focused on helping people and organizations design, evaluate, and deploy intelligent systems that amplify human capability.

Its work spans foundational research, production systems, enterprise AI transformation, and the development of a domain-independent language for describing Human–AI interaction.

Mission

Human–AI interaction should adapt to humans.

h/ai is an independent initiative exploring technologies, systems, and authoring environments that make AI interaction more natural, continuous, contextual, and human.

The focus is not replacing people. The focus is amplifying them, individually and collectively, through more natural forms of human-AI collaboration.

Transformation

AI Transformation

Organizations increasingly recognize the potential of AI, but selecting technologies is only one part of successful transformation. h/ai works with organizations to evaluate emerging AI capabilities, identify meaningful opportunities, design Human–AI interaction systems, and guide production deployment through a vendor-neutral approach.

AI Strategy

Helping organizations identify where Human–AI interaction creates meaningful operational value.

Technology Evaluation

Vendor-neutral evaluation of conversational AI, speech, avatars, orchestration, and multimodal platforms.

Solution Architecture

Designing production-ready Human–AI systems around customer workflows rather than individual technologies.

Implementation & Adoption

Supporting deployment, organizational adoption, governance, and continuous improvement.

Technology rarely fails because of technology. It fails because organizations struggle to evaluate, integrate, and adopt it.

h/ai exists to bridge that gap.

Interrupting an Avatar

Human conversation is naturally interruptible. Conversational systems should preserve continuity while allowing people to redirect the interaction at any time.

An Avatar Needs To Remember Its Place

Interruptions should never force a conversation to start over. Context, visual state, and conversational progress should be restored exactly where they were left.

An Avatar Needs To Follow Your Lead

People, not software, should determine the direction of a conversation. AI should adapt continuously as human priorities change.

An Avatar Needs To Catch What You Miss

A conversational system should recognize missing information, ask the same clarifying questions an experienced employee would, and improve the quality of an interaction before committing to action.

h/ai PRIMITIVE LANGUAGE

A Domain-Independent Language for Human–AI Interaction

The h/ai Primitive Language is a domain-independent vocabulary for describing Human–AI interaction. Rather than describing specific implementations or technologies, the framework models interaction itself through a collection of fundamental primitives.

Those primitives are organized into complementary Primitive Families that describe how interaction unfolds through time, how information is represented and communicated, and how intelligent systems reason, plan, and act. Together, these families provide a common language for describing Human–AI interaction across conversational systems, education, robotics, accessibility, adaptive interfaces, authoring systems, and other interactive environments.

Three Primitive Families

The Primitive Language is intentionally modular. Each primitive belongs to one of three complementary families. Together these families form a foundational description of Human–AI interaction while remaining independent enough to evolve individually over time.

Foundational Principle

Human–AI interaction can be described independently of any implementation technology. The h/ai Primitive Language defines that interaction through a small collection of orthogonal primitives organized into Temporal, Information, and Behavioral families. Complex interaction emerges from combinations of simple primitives.

Active Research

Questions currently being explored.

The following areas are active investigations within h/ai and should be considered exploratory directions rather than formal components of the current framework. Many of these questions emerge directly from observing how humans naturally communicate, collaborate, delegate, and make decisions in shared environments.

Multi-Participant Interaction

Most conversational systems assume a one-human, one-AI interaction model. Real-world interaction rarely operates this way. Families, classrooms, teams, and communities often communicate simultaneously, interrupt one another, contribute partial information, and collaborate toward shared outcomes. h/ai is exploring interaction models for these environments.

Authority and Delegation

In many human interactions, participants contribute information while a designated individual retains decision authority. Research is underway into conversational systems that can recognize contribution, preserve intent, and route decisions through the appropriate participant.

Shared Interaction Spaces

Future interaction systems may operate within classrooms, meetings, homes, vehicles, and public spaces where multiple humans and AI systems participate simultaneously. h/ai is exploring interaction models for these environments.

It’ll be especially valuable at distinguishing between surface-level knowledge and genuine understanding.

Beyond Access

Melinda Medina, M.Ed., M.S. · AI-Enabled Learning & Workforce Capability Strategist

Many organizations no longer struggle with access to AI tools. The greater challenge is integrating those tools into workflows, learning experiences, and decision-making processes in ways that meaningfully improve performance.

Question: If access to AI becomes ubiquitous, what organizational, instructional, and workflow changes are required for AI to become meaningfully integrated into daily practice rather than simply available as a tool?
