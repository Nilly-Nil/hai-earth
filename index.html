Mission

AI should adapt to humans.

H/AI is an independent initiative exploring technologies, systems, and authoring environments that make AI interaction more natural, continuous, contextual, and human.

The focus is not replacing people. The focus is amplifying them, individually and collectively, through more natural forms of human-AI collaboration.

Active Research

Questions currently being explored.

The following areas are active investigations within H/AI and should be considered exploratory directions rather than formal components of the current framework. Many of these questions emerge directly from observing how humans naturally communicate, collaborate, delegate, and make decisions in shared environments.

Multi-Participant Interaction

Most conversational systems assume a one-human, one-AI interaction model. Real-world interaction rarely operates this way. Families, classrooms, teams, and communities often communicate simultaneously, interrupt one another, contribute partial information, and collaborate toward shared outcomes. H/AI is exploring interaction models for these environments.

Authority and Delegation

In many human interactions, participants contribute information while a designated individual retains decision authority. Research is underway into conversational systems that can recognize contribution, preserve intent, and route decisions through the appropriate participant.

Shared Interaction Spaces

Future interaction systems may operate within classrooms, meetings, homes, vehicles, and public spaces where multiple humans and AI systems participate simultaneously. H/AI is exploring interaction models for these environments.

H/AI Framework

Primitive Language — Draft v1

The H/AI Primitive Language describes how human interaction is organized, traversed, interrupted, resumed, committed, and remembered across time.

The framework is intentionally domain-independent and is designed to model persistent human/AI interaction across conversational systems, education, travel planning, adaptive interfaces, authoring systems, and long-duration interactive experiences.

Draft v1 Published June 3, 2026

Timestamp

A unique temporal identifier associated with a Traversal Point.

Domain

The contextual space within which interaction occurs.

Sub-domain

A specialization of a Domain providing additional contextual precision.

State

The complete condition of a Domain and Sub-domain immediately following mutation.

Traversal Point

A timestamped State representing a unique location within Traversal.

Traversal

The sequential ordering of Traversal Points progressing forward through time.

Command

An instruction that causes State mutation and initiates traversal activity.

Interruption

A non-linear progression that departs from the active Traversal.

Resumption

Continuation of Traversal generated from a previously recorded Traversal Point.

Commitment Point

A Traversal Point at which future mutation produces external consequence.

External Action

A consequence-producing operation affecting external systems or real-world outcomes.

Foundational Principle

Any mutation of State within a Domain or Sub-domain receives a Timestamp and creates a new Traversal Point. Ordered Traversal Points form Traversal. Interaction history, continuity, interruption, resumption, and provenance emerge from navigation through Traversal Points over time.
