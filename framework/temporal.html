H/AI Primitive Language

Volume V
Temporal Primitives

Temporal primitives describe how Human–AI interaction is organized, traversed, interrupted, resumed, committed, and remembered across time.

Draft v1.2Volume VTemporal Structure

Overview

The temporal family defines the structure of interaction as it unfolds. It provides a vocabulary for locating an interaction in time, recording state change, preserving continuity, and returning to prior interaction positions after interruption.

These primitives are intentionally domain-independent. They can describe conversational systems, adaptive interfaces, educational sequences, reservation workflows, readings, visual experiences, task execution, and long-duration Human–AI interaction.

Primitive Definitions

Timestamp

A unique temporal identifier associated with a Traversal Point.

Sequence

The ordered relationship between Traversal Points, defining the progression of interaction independent of elapsed time.

Duration

The measurable temporal interval associated with a State, Traversal, or interaction between two Traversal Points.

Transition

Movement from one State or Domain to another during Traversal.

Traversal

The sequential ordering of Traversal Points progressing forward through time.

Commitment

A Traversal Point at which future mutation produces external consequence.

Foundational Principle

Any mutation of State within a Domain or Sub-domain receives a Timestamp and creates a new Traversal Point. Ordered Traversal Points form Traversal. Interaction history, continuity, interruption, resumption, and provenance emerge from navigation through Traversal Points over time.

Interaction Example

A reading is active. A user interrupts to make a restaurant reservation. The system preserves the reading state, enters the reservation domain, completes the reservation, then resumes the reading from the exact Traversal Point where it was interrupted.
